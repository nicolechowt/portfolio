Who am I?

Me - In a nutshell.

Hardware Skills

With a degree and license in Electrical Engineering, needless to say, I love hardware. I love physically putting together parts and building something amazing with my very own hands. One of my proudest projects includes a smart biker's vest that would activate the turn and stop signals according to body motions. My friends and I have also built and presented a mini smart-city at our college's symposium.

Software Skills

JavaScript, UX Design, React, Node, Express,d3, MySQL, MongoDB, RESTful APIs, AJAX, Firebase, Heroku, Handlebars.js, MongoDB, Redux, Materialize, JIRA, Git, Github, jQuery, bootstrap, HTML, CSS.

I didn't discover my passion for software until 2014. I began my journey writing software for theme park attractions. A few years later, I discovered Web Dev. I love how Web Dev perfectly marries tech and art together. I am especially interested in...

-anything IoT (hey, software AND hardware!)
-fitness/health-tech
-education-related projects

Creative Entrepreneurship

My best friend and I started a floral business at age 16. I started and re-started a wedding photography business in 2012 and 2014. Though I have shifted my focus at the moment, I have always had an entrepreneurial spirit in me.

Diversity in Tech

I almost didn't pursue tech because of the lack of women in the field. I regularly reach out to young girls at local middle and high schools to speak with them about my experience in STEM. I also volunteer at local women-in-tech events.

People Skills

Having worked at one of the largest entertainment companies in the world, I've had the privledge of working alongside the smartest and most creative people. I have learned to adapt the different communication styles. I take pride in my ability to empathaize and relate. I have developed an excellent working relationship with those in engineering, maintenance, operations, design and senior management.

Experience Design

I have found the psychology of product interaction to be fascinating. I want to know why users make the decisions they do, and why they preceive things a certain way. This way of thinking has allowed me to design software that is aligned with both the business and users' end goals.
